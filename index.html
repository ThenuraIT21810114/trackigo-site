Research Problem & Solution

Problem Statement

Public transport systems face delays, breakdowns, and inefficient scheduling due to fragmented monitoring and outdated static timetables.

Crowding, inaccurate ETAs, and reactive maintenance result in poor commuter experiences. Authorities lack a unified, predictive platform that can forecast demand, pre-empt faults, and coordinate service recovery in real time.

  • Disjoint GPS and ticketing systems ‚Üí no synchronized visibility
  • Static schedules ‚Üí failure to adapt to real-time traffic demand
  • Reactive maintenance ‚Üí unplanned downtime and poor reliability

Proposed Solution

  • Live Ops: real-time route monitoring, occupancy & headway control.
  • Forecasting: LSTM/ARIMA + XGBoost for short-term demand prediction.
  • Predictive Maintenance: IoT telemetry + anomaly detection models.
  • Automated Response: instant alerts with recommended recovery actions.
  • Cloud Scale: containerized microservices deployed on AWS/GCP.

Pilot test results: < 5s location latency ‚Ä¢ ~86% demand forecast accuracy ‚Ä¢ up to ~30% reduction in downtime.
